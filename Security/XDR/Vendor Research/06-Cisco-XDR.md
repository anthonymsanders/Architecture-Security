## Key Capabilities

- **Network-centric XDR**: Deep visibility from Cisco's networking stack (firewalls, switches, routers, SD-WAN)
- **Talos Threat Intelligence**: One of the largest commercial threat intelligence teams
- **Identity integration**: Duo MFA and ISE for identity-based detection and response
- **IoT/OT visibility**: Cyber Vision for industrial and IoT device monitoring
- **Cisco AI Assistant**: AI-powered security assistant for investigation and response
- **Risk-based prioritization**: Prioritizes alerts based on asset criticality and context

## Strengths

- Strong network-centric detection leveraging Cisco's networking dominance
- Native identity capabilities (Duo, ISE)
- Talos threat intelligence is world-class
- Broad networking/security portfolio integration
- Excellent IoT/OT visibility via Cyber Vision
- Good for organizations already invested in Cisco infrastructure

## Weaknesses

- Endpoint detection depth lags behind CrowdStrike, SentinelOne, Palo Alto
- Newer XDR platform (launched 2023); less mature than established XDR leaders
- Integration complexity across Cisco's broad portfolio
- Cloud security capabilities still evolving
- Limited SIEM convergence story
- Historically complex licensing model

## Ideal For

- Cisco networking/security stack customers
- Network-heavy environments needing deep network visibility
- Organizations with significant IoT/OT infrastructure
- Enterprises wanting to leverage existing Cisco investment
